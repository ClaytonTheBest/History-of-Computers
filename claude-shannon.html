Claude Shannon

Boole became an expert on what some scientists call the most complicated thing in the universe. The human brain. Booles research on logic and connecting algebra laid the framework for the information age. Now his work existed, but wasn't actually incorperated in a computer until someone named Claude Shannon learned about Boolean algebra. After learning about boolean algebra, Claude connected it to laying electrical switching circuits. The connection of the binary to the logic of boolean algebra has become involved in every computer ever.
